Meet Your Coach

Hello, I’m Shannon

“Healing didn’t come from forcing it — it came from learning how to listen to my body.”

After my divorce, I remember feeling exhausted and lost, unsure how to keep moving forward when my heart felt shattered. On the outside I was functioning. On the inside, my body was bracing, holding its breath, trying to survive what it had just lost.

I did not need more advice. I needed my body to feel safe again. Through my own healing, I discovered that what truly helped was not pushing harder or thinking my way through the pain, but learning how to work with my nervous system.

Today, I support women around the world who are navigating heartbreak or divorce and want a different way to heal — one that doesn’t rush the process, suppress emotions, or keep them stuck.

📖 How to Publish Your Site
Follow these steps every time you want your changes to go live on all devices worldwide.
Why do I need to Export and Upload?

Changes you make here save to this browser on this computer only — like editing a Word doc on your desktop. GitHub is the real live website that everyone in the world sees. The Export & Download button bakes all your changes into a file you then upload to GitHub to publish worldwide.

Step-by-Step Publishing Guide
1
Make your changes in the CMS
Update text, images, links, social URLs, pricing in the sidebar sections. Changes auto-save and update the page live as you type.
2
Click 💾 Save
Press Save in the top bar to commit all fields to storage. A confirmation will appear at the bottom of the screen.
3
Click ⇓ Export & Download
This downloads a file called index.html with all your changes permanently baked in. This is the file you upload to GitHub.
4
Go to GitHub → Open your repository
Open a new tab, go to github.com, log in, and click on your repository (e.g. shannon-dawn-healing).
5
Click index.html → pencil ✏ icon
Click on index.html in the file list, then click the pencil edit icon in the top right of the file preview to open the editor.
6
Select all (Ctrl+A) → Delete → Paste new code
Click inside the editor, press Ctrl+A (Cmd+A on Mac) to select all, delete it, then open your downloaded index.html in Notepad, select all, copy, and paste it into GitHub.
7
Click "Commit changes" → Wait 2-3 minutes
Scroll down and click the green Commit changes button. GitHub Pages rebuilds automatically. After 2-3 minutes your changes are live on every phone, tablet and browser worldwide. ✦
